The Battle of Vienna took place on September 12, 1683 near Kahlenberg Mountain, after the capital had been besieged by the Ottoman Empire for two months. Polish-Austrian-German forces led by King of Poland John III Sobieski won the battle against the Ottoman Empire army commanded by Grand Vizier Merzifonlu Kara Mustafa Pasha. The battle marked the turning point in the 300-year struggle between the forces of the Central European kingdoms fighting against the Ottoman Empire.

The Battle of Shiroyama took place on 24 September 1877, at Kagoshima, Japan. It was the final battle of the Satsuma Rebellion, where the heavily outnumbered Samurai under Saigo Takamori made their last stand against Imperial Japanese Army troops under the command of generals Yamagata Aritomo and Kawamura Sumiyoshi. The battle culminated in the annihilation of Saigo's army as well as his death, marking the end of the Satsuma Rebellion.
